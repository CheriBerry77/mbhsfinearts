Academics

Course Listings

Explore our offerings across visual arts, music, theatre, and dance. 1 Fine Arts credit is required for graduation. Courses marked with * have multiple levels and carry advanced weight toward your GPA.

Music · ART2076 A/B
AP Music Theory
Mrs. Isabel Hernandez-Cata
Learn to recognize, understand, and describe the basic materials and processes of music. Develop skills by listening to, reading, writing, and performing a wide variety of music. Modeled on College Board AP guidelines.
Music · ART2071–2074 A/B *
Piano 1–4 *
Mrs. Isabel Hernandez-Cata
No experience needed for Piano 1 — great for beginners who want to learn how to play an instrument. Four progressive levels match all experience ranges, from first-time players to advanced pianists.
Music · ART2082–2081 A/B *
Guitar 1–4 *
Mr. Christopher Mercado
An introduction to notes, rhythms, chords, scales, music theory, and songwriting. Students leave able to play full songs across genres, read guitar sheet music, perform solos, improvise riffs, and compose basic pieces.
Music · ART2092 A/B
World Drumming
Mr. Raife Oldham
An exploration of percussion traditions from around the world, covering drumming techniques, rhythmic styles, and the cultural contexts of music from Africa, Latin America, and beyond.
Music · ART2084 A/B
Music Technology
Ms. Michelle Roberts
Create your own drum patterns, bass lines, chord progressions, and melodies using tools including Audacity, Sibelius, GarageBand, and Reason. Topics span music production, copyright law, acoustics, and recording studio techniques.
Music · ART2093 / 2094 / 2154 A/B *
Band (Concert / Symphonic / Wind Ensemble) *
Mr. Raife Oldham
Three leveled ensembles for wind, brass, and percussion players. Students develop technical proficiency, ensemble listening, and performance skills across a broad repertoire from marches to contemporary concert works.
Music · ART2097 / 2099 / 2155 A/B *
Orchestra (Concert / Symphonic / Philharmonic) *
Ms. Michelle Roberts
Three leveled orchestras for string and full ensemble players, exploring orchestral literature from Baroque through modern eras. Students build technical mastery, sight-reading, and ensemble artistry through regular performance.
Music · ART2100 A/B
Jazz Ensemble
Ms. Michelle Roberts
An ensemble focused on jazz performance, improvisation, and the traditions of the genre. Students explore big band literature, small-group jazz styles, and develop improvisational vocabulary through active ensemble playing.
Music · ART2087–2090 / 2091 A/B *
Chorus 1–3, Show Choir & Chamber Singers *
Mrs. Isabel Hernandez-Cata
No experience needed for Chorus 1 — students just need to love to sing. Auditions are required for advanced levels. Show Choir and Chamber Singers match students to higher-level vocal and performance challenges.
Theatre · ART2106–2107 A/B *
Theatre 1–2 *
Theatre Department
Units include improvisation, scene studies, musical theatre, stage combat, world theatre history, and silent film. A new ESOL Theatre 1 section is also available for English language learners.
Theatre · ART2103 A/B
Advanced Acting
Theatre Department
Builds on skills from Theatre 1 & 2. Includes advanced monologue and scene work, script analysis, and directing for stage and screen. Emphasis on developing a personal acting process and artistic voice.
Theatre · ART2104 A/B
Stage Design
Theatre Department
An exploration of scenic design, set construction, lighting, and the visual elements of theatrical production. Students develop design concepts and learn the technical skills needed to bring a production to life.
Theatre · ART2105 A/B
Play Directing
Theatre Department
Students study and practice the craft of directing for stage and screen, including script analysis, working with actors, staging, and the director's role in the full production process.
Dance · ART2000 A/B
Dance as a Fine Art
Dance Department
This introductory course develops technique and explores dance as a fine art. Students learn basic technical skills across multiple disciplines and the history of dance in many cultures. Dance B deepens these elements through original choreography and improvisation. May be repeated for elective credit.
Visual Art · ART2054 A/B
AP Art History
Visual Art Department
Learn about 5,000 years of art history from ancient art through the 21st century. Modeled on College Board guidelines and AP testing requirements, this course develops visual analysis and critical thinking skills.
Visual Art · ART2064 A/B
AP Studio Art 2D
Visual Art Department
Develop a body of work based on your own theme. A year-long course culminating in a portfolio of 20 images that demonstrate finished artworks, artistic processes, revision, and experimentation.
Visual Art · ART2040 A/B
AP Photography
Visual Art Department
An advanced portfolio-based course in which students develop a cohesive body of photographic work around a self-directed theme, submitted as a 20-image portfolio for AP evaluation.
Visual Art · ART2001 A/B
Foundations of Art & Culture
Visual Art Department
Learn and create a wide variety of artworks using many different mediums, including drawing, painting, crafts, and sculpture. A great entry point for students new to visual art.
Visual Art · ART2053 / 2049 A/B
Functional Fine Art & Craft
Visual Art Department
Learn and create with traditional and contemporary materials including basketry, fibers, glass, jewelry, metal, mixed media, paper, pottery, wearable art, and wood. An Advanced FFAC section is also offered.
Visual Art · ART2022 A/B
Fashion Illustration
Visual Art Department
Explore contemporary fashion and fabric design using various materials and techniques. Develop an understanding of the fashion industry and the range of career opportunities it offers.
Visual Art · ART2038–2035 / 2040 A/B *
Photography 1–4 & AP *
Visual Art Department
Learn to take stunning photographs regardless of camera type. Explore aperture, shutter speed, ISO, composition, analog film/darkroom photography, digital photography, and Photoshop. Five levels from beginner to AP.
Visual Art · ART2045–2048 A/B *
Ceramics & Sculpture 1–4 *
Visual Art Department
Learn hand-building skills while working in clay and other 3D mediums to create art. Four progressive levels develop technique from foundational coil and slab methods through advanced wheel-throwing and sculptural forms.
Visual Art · ART2069–2060 A/B *
Digital Art 1–4 *
Visual Art Department
Create art using the Adobe Creative Suite (Illustrator, Photoshop, InDesign). Projects span collage, drawing, illustration, portrait, typography, photomanipulation, publication, painting, animation, and product design.
Visual Art · ART2061 A/B
Animation (Advanced)
Visual Art Department
Create animations using Adobe Animate and the Adobe Creative Suite. Projects include rotoscoping, traditional animation, stop motion, claymation, and motion graphics.
Visual Art · ART2042–2033 A/B *
Studio Art 1–4 *
Visual Art Department
Learn observational drawing using graphite, colored pencil, charcoal, pastel, ink, acrylic, watercolor, tempera, collage, and mixed media. Themes include portraiture, still life, landscape, illustration, realism, and abstraction.
Visual Art · ART2043 A/B
Painting
Visual Art Department
Learn to paint landscapes, portraits, still life, abstraction, and more using tempera, acrylic, watercolor, and mixed media. Themes explore dotilism, realism, and personal perception.
Visual Art · ART2044 A/B
Printmaking
Visual Art Department
Learn a unique art form that transfers images from one surface to another. Techniques include lino printing, engraving, stamp-making, and screen printing to create a variety of original printed works.
