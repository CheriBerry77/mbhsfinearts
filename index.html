Academics

Course Listings

Click any course card to expand its full description. Courses marked * have multiple levels and carry advanced weight toward your GPA. 1 Fine Arts credit is required for graduation.

Music · ART2076 A/B
AP Music Theory
Mrs. Isabel Hernandez-Cata
▸ Click for full description
Learn to recognize, understand, and describe the basic materials and processes of music. Develop skills by listening to, reading, writing, and performing a wide variety of music. Modeled on College Board AP guidelines.

Contact: isabel_hernandez-cata@mcpsmd.org
Music · ART2071–2074 A/B *
Piano 1–4 *
Mrs. Isabel Hernandez-Cata
▸ Click for full description
No experience needed for Piano 1 — great for beginners. Four progressive levels accommodate all experience ranges from first-time players to advanced pianists.

Contact: isabel_hernandez-cata@mcpsmd.org
Music · ART2082–2081 A/B *
Guitar 1–4 *
Mr. Christopher Mercado
▸ Click for full description
Introduction to notes, rhythms, chords, scales, music theory, and songwriting. Students leave able to play full songs across genres, read guitar sheet music, perform solos, improvise riffs, and compose basic pieces.

Contact: christopher_m_mercado@mcpsmd.org
Music · ART2092 A/B
World Drumming
Mr. Raife Oldham
▸ Click for full description
Exploration of percussion traditions from around the world, covering drumming techniques, rhythmic styles, and the cultural contexts of music from Africa, Latin America, and beyond.

Contact: raife_e_oldham@mcpsmd.org
Music · ART2084 A/B
Music Technology
Ms. Michelle Roberts
▸ Click for full description
Create drum patterns, bass lines, chord progressions, and melodies using Audacity, Sibelius, GarageBand, and Reason. Topics include music production, copyright law, acoustics, and recording studio techniques.

Contact: michelle_roberts@mcpsmd.org
Music · ART2093 / 2094 / 2154 A/B *
Band — Concert / Symphonic / Wind Ensemble *
Mr. Raife Oldham
▸ Click for full description
Three leveled ensembles for wind, brass, and percussion players. Students develop technical proficiency, ensemble listening, and performance skills across a broad repertoire from marches to contemporary works.

Contact: raife_e_oldham@mcpsmd.org
Music · ART2097 / 2099 / 2155 A/B *
Orchestra — Concert / Symphonic / Philharmonic *
Ms. Michelle Roberts
▸ Click for full description
Three leveled orchestras for string and full ensemble players, exploring literature from Baroque through modern eras. Builds technical mastery, sight-reading, and ensemble artistry through regular performance.

Contact: michelle_roberts@mcpsmd.org
Music · ART2100 A/B
Jazz Ensemble
Ms. Michelle Roberts
▸ Click for full description
Focused on jazz performance, improvisation, and the traditions of the genre. Students explore big band literature, small-group styles, and develop improvisational vocabulary through active ensemble playing.

Contact: michelle_roberts@mcpsmd.org
Music · ART2087–2090 / 2091 A/B *
Chorus 1–3, Show Choir & Chamber Singers *
Mrs. Isabel Hernandez-Cata
▸ Click for full description
No experience needed for Chorus 1. Auditions required for advanced levels, Show Choir, and Chamber Singers. Students develop vocal technique, music literacy, and choral blend.

Contact: isabel_hernandez-cata@mcpsmd.org
Theatre · ART2106–2107 A/B *
Theatre 1–2 *
Theatre Department
▸ Click for full description
Units include improvisation, scene studies, musical theatre, stage combat, world theatre history, and silent film. A new ESOL Theatre 1 section is available for English language learners.
Theatre · ART2103 A/B
Advanced Acting
Theatre Department
▸ Click for full description
Builds on Theatre 1 & 2. Covers advanced monologue and scene work, script analysis, and directing for stage and screen, with emphasis on developing a personal acting process.
Theatre · ART2104 A/B
Stage Design
Theatre Department
▸ Click for full description
Covers scenic design, set construction, lighting, and the visual elements of theatrical production. Students develop design concepts and learn technical skills needed to bring a production to life.
Theatre · ART2105 A/B
Play Directing
Theatre Department
▸ Click for full description
Students study and practice directing for stage and screen, including script analysis, working with actors, staging, and the director's role in the overall production process.
Dance · ART2000 A/B
Dance as a Fine Art
Dance Department
▸ Click for full description
Introductory course developing technique across multiple dance disciplines and the history of dance in many cultures. Dance B deepens these elements through original choreography and improvisation. May be repeated for elective credit.
Visual Art · ART2054 A/B
AP Art History
Visual Art Department
▸ Click for full description
Covers 5,000 years of art history from ancient through 21st-century art. Modeled on College Board AP guidelines and testing requirements. Develops visual analysis and critical thinking skills.
Visual Art · ART2064 A/B
AP Studio Art 2D
Visual Art Department
▸ Click for full description
Year-long course in which students develop a body of work around a self-chosen theme. Final submission is a portfolio of 20 images showing finished artworks, artistic processes, revision, and experimentation.
Visual Art · ART2040 A/B
AP Photography
Visual Art Department
▸ Click for full description
Advanced portfolio-based photography course. Students develop a cohesive body of work around a self-directed theme, submitted as a 20-image portfolio for AP evaluation.
Visual Art · ART2001 A/B
Foundations of Art & Culture
Visual Art Department
▸ Click for full description
Create a wide variety of artworks using many mediums including drawing, painting, crafts, and sculpture. A strong entry-point course for students new to visual art.
Visual Art · ART2053 / 2049 A/B
Functional Fine Art & Craft
Visual Art Department
▸ Click for full description
Work with traditional and contemporary materials including basketry, fibers, glass, jewelry, metal, mixed media, paper, pottery, wearable art, and wood. An Advanced FFAC section is also offered.
Visual Art · ART2022 A/B
Fashion Illustration
Visual Art Department
▸ Click for full description
Explore contemporary fashion and fabric design using various materials and techniques. Develop an understanding of the fashion industry and the career opportunities within it.
Visual Art · ART2038–2035 / 2040 A/B *
Photography 1–4 & AP *
Visual Art Department
▸ Click for full description
Learn photography regardless of camera type. Covers aperture, shutter speed, ISO, composition, analog film/darkroom photography, digital photography, and Photoshop. Five levels from beginner to AP.
Visual Art · ART2045–2048 A/B *
Ceramics & Sculpture 1–4 *
Visual Art Department
▸ Click for full description
Learn hand-building in clay and other 3D mediums. Four levels progress from foundational coil and slab methods through advanced wheel-throwing and sculptural forms.
Visual Art · ART2069–2060 A/B *
Digital Art 1–4 *
Visual Art Department
▸ Click for full description
Create art using Adobe Illustrator, Photoshop, and InDesign. Projects span collage, drawing, illustration, portrait, typography, photomanipulation, publication, painting, animation, and product design.
Visual Art · ART2061 A/B
Animation (Advanced)
Visual Art Department
▸ Click for full description
Create animations using Adobe Animate and the Adobe Creative Suite. Projects include rotoscoping, traditional animation, stop motion, claymation, and motion graphics.
Visual Art · ART2042–2033 A/B *
Studio Art 1–4 *
Visual Art Department
▸ Click for full description
Learn observational drawing using graphite, colored pencil, charcoal, pastel, ink, acrylic, watercolor, tempera, collage, and mixed media. Themes include portraiture, still life, landscape, illustration, realism, and abstraction.
Visual Art · ART2043 A/B
Painting
Visual Art Department
▸ Click for full description
Paint landscapes, portraits, still life, abstraction, and more using tempera, acrylic, watercolor, and mixed media. Themes include dotilism, realism, and personal perception.
Visual Art · ART2044 A/B
Printmaking
Visual Art Department
▸ Click for full description
Learn the art of transferring images from one surface to another. Techniques include lino printing, engraving, stamp-making, and screen printing.
